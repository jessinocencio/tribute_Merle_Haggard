Merle Haggard

Guitarist, Songwriter, Singer
(1937-2016)

Timeline

  • 1937

    Born near Bakersfield, California.

  • 1958

    Sent to San Quentin prison at the age of 20 after being convicted for burglary and attempted escape from county jail. While serving a 2 1/2-year term, he played in the prison's country band and took high school equivalency courses.

  • 1962

    Signed with a small label, Tally Records. Recorded debut single "Sing a Sad Song" (number 19 on the country charts).

  • 1967-1969

    Released six number one singles, such as "Branded Man", "Mama Tried" and "Workin' Man Blues". His song "Okie from Muskogee", which Haggard wrote in response to the American protests against Vietnam War, achieved widespread popularity and acclaim.

  • 1970

    Earned the Country Music Association's Awards for Single, Entertainer and Top Male Vocalist of Year. The album of the same name also won Album of the Year.

  • 1981

    Published autobiography "Sing Me Back Home".

  • 1994

    Inducted into the Country Music Hall of Fame for artistic achievements, including 38 number one hits.

  • 2016

    Died on April 6, 2016 (his 79th birthday) at home on his Northern California Ranch due to complications from pneumonia.
